Bio

I obtained my international Ph.D. degree in mathematics in September 2017 from the University of the Basque Country (UPV-EHU, Spain) and the University of Pau (UPPA, France) under Prof. David Pardo (UPV-EHU) and Prof. Hélène Barucq (UPPA) co-supervision. The best possible distinction awarded me: Cum Laude. The thesis thematic was on error representations for mesh p-adaptivity for Goal-Oriented Problems applied to Helmholtz problems. Until June 2019, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher in Bilbao with Prof. David Pardo in the MathMode project. We developed a new hp-adaptive process. I continued my postdoctoral formation in Cerfacs, Toulouse, until June 2020. I worked together with Dr. Carola Kruse and members of the Algo Team. We focused on Structural Mechanics problems provided by our collaborator Dr. Nicolas Tardieu at the French energy producer EDF. I am currently a Post-doc researcher at IRIT in the APO team in Toulouse within the EoCoE II European project. I collaborate with Dr. Alfredo Buttari and Dr. Pierre Jolivet on Domain Decomposition Technics and inexact direct Solvers.

Painless hp-adaptivity

We implement our method on top of Zander et al. hierarchical data-structure. Our main contribution resides in the adaptive strategy. For a given mesh, we aim at returning the coarsest discretization possible without overly increasing the error.

In this paper, we describe the algorithm and apply it to Symmetric Positive Definite problems (manufactured solutions for a Laplace equation). The adaptive algorithm is the following:

  1. Start with an initial coarse mesh
  2. Refine it (user-defined refinement, eg. global h-refinements and/or global p-refinements)
  3. Perform the coarsening step
  4. Return to step 1 until the mesh is fine enough and the error below the threshold
The unrefinement step proceeds as follows:
  1. Compute the solution
  2. Identify the removable basis functions
  3. Evaluate their contribution
  4. Remove the least contributing ones
We illustrate the process in the following animation:

To build the hp-coarsening step, we need a criteria to know if we should proceed to h- or p-unrefinements. For a given element,

  • If its contribution is small compare to the average of contributions then proceed to p-unrefinement,

  • if the contribution of its siblings are also small, then perform an h-unrefinement instead.
